Knoxvillians

The people of Knoxville, Tennessee, are known for their warm hospitality and strong sense of community. With a population of approximately 190,000 residents, Knoxvillians take pride in their city's rich cultural heritage and embrace the region's diverse traditions and backgrounds. Visitors often comment on the friendly and welcoming nature of the locals, creating a warm and inviting atmosphere for both newcomers and long-time residents. Knoxville's residents are deeply passionate about their city's history and take an active interest in preserving its cultural landmarks and heritage. The city's strong sense of community is evident in the numerous local festivals, events, and gatherings that bring people together to celebrate their shared roots. Whether it's enjoying live music performances at the historic Market Square or cheering on the University of Tennessee Volunteers at a football game, Knoxvillians revel in fostering a strong bond that unites the city's residents as one big family. Moreover, Knoxville's people are renowned for their enthusiasm for the outdoors and appreciation for nature's beauty. Surrounded by the stunning Smoky Mountains and nestled along the Tennessee River, residents frequently engage in outdoor activities such as hiking, kayaking, and biking, making the most of the region's natural wonders. This passion for the great outdoors creates a healthy and active lifestyle that further contributes to the city's vibrant and engaging community.

Knoxville is situated next to Great Smoky Mountains National Park, the most visited national park in America


Knoxville is 889 feet above sea level


Knoxville is accessible from an international waterway
